Responsible Authorities:  Vice President, Research Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee Director, Research Integrity Director, Comparative Medicine

  1. Background

    In accordance with the PHS Policy on Humane Care and Use of Laboratory Animals and theAnimal Welfare Act all personnel using animals in research, teaching, or testing as well as individuals having occupational exposure to animals are required to participate in an occupational health program.

  2. Purpose

    The purpose of this policy is to provide guidance to Research Personnel regarding the Occupational Health Program at FAU for animal users.

  3. General Statement

    FAU’s IACUC is responsible for ensuring that all animal users working with or having occupational exposure to animals are in compliance with the federal regulations, including the Animal Welfare Act, the Public Health Service Policy (PHS) and are consistent with the Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals.

  4. Policy

    FAU’s Occupational Health Program for animal users is coordinated through Environmental Health and Safety (EH&S) and it is entitled the Medical Monitoring Program for Animal Users. All animal users or individuals with occupational exposure to research animals are required to participate in this program. The program description and enrollment forms may be found at https://www.fau.edu/facilities/ehs/info/Medical-Monitoring-Animal-Use.pdf

  5. Definitions

    1. Research personnel includes anybody who is working with animals and listed on the IACUC protocol. It includes but is not limited to the following individuals:

      1. Scientists

      2. Research technicians

      3. Fellows, residents and post-docs

      4. Students including undergraduate and graduate students

      5. Visiting scholars

      6. Volunteers

      7. PIs and Co-PIs on IACUC protocols, even if those individuals will not have direct animal contact

  6. Accountability

      The Principal Investigator (PI) will be responsible for:

    • Assuring that all personnel using animals in research, teaching, or testing as well as individuals having occupational exposure to animals participate in FAU’s occupational health program prior to commencing activities with animals.

      The IACUC will be responsible for:

    • Providing oversight for all animal care and use procedures conducted.

    • Reviewing and approving, requiring modifications in (to secure approval) or withholding approval of IACUC protocols and/or amendments.

    • Assuring that all personnel conducting work with animals are appropriately trained and have enrolled in FAU’s Medical Monitoring Program for Animal Users.

      • The Research Integrity (RI) / IACUC office will be responsible for:

    • Administrative support of the IACUC members to facilitate their regulatory function.

    • Maintaining policy and assure regular review and update as necessary by the IACUC.

    The Office of Comparative Medicine (CM) will be responsible for:

    • Ensuring all Comparative Medicine personnel are enrolled in the Occupational Health Program.

  7. Procedures

    All animal users or personnel with occupational exposure to research animals must enroll in FAU’s Medical Monitoring Program for Animal Users. Enrollment is via a form submitted to EH&S for review. The program description and enrollment forms may be found at https://www.fau.edu/facilities/ehs/info/Medical-Monitoring-Animal- Use.pdf. Animal users will not be approved as project personnel on an IACUC protocol until they are enrolled in the program. New protocols will not be approved until all personnel listed on the protocol are enrolled in the program.

The University...at a Glance

Some universities measure greatness in decades and centuries. Florida Atlantic University measures it with every student who earns a degree, every researcher who makes a discovery and every community that is transformed.

At FAU's dedication ceremony in 1964, President Lyndon B. Johnson challenged the University's pioneers: "It is time now...for a new, adventurous, imaginative, courageous breakthrough for a new revolution in education in America."

With those words, FAU opened its doors as the first public university in southeast Florida and the first in America designed for upper division students only. Since day one, FAU has pushed the bounds of higher education. Now, 50 years later, the University serves more than 30,000 freshmen, transfers and graduate students at sites throughout its six-county service region in southeast Florida.

People from every walk of life find a place at FAU. Students choose from more than 170 degree programs, faculty researchers utilize more than 40 research centers and the community engages hundreds of cultural and educational events every year.
