  1. Background

    Florida Atlantic University (FAU) is committed to the humane care and use of animals inresearch, training,andexhibitionactivities.TheUniversityhasadoptedinstitution- wide animal care standards that are based on the Animal Welfare Act regulations and PublicHealthService(PHS) Policy,andhasaformaldocumentonfile(an“Animal WelfareAssurance”)withtheOfficefor LaboratoryAnimalWelfare(OLAW)to documentitscommitmenttothesestandards.The overarchinggoaloftheseeffortsis toensurethatallactivitiesinvolvinganimalsareconductedina humaneandethical manner, that housing and care of animals is appropriate for the species and to ensure that faculty, staff, and students working with animals are safe and protected.

  2. Purpose

    This document outlines the administrative structure of the FAU animal care and use program. These administrative offices and leaders interact with and provide resources to the many faculty, staff, and students who work with animals in their research, training, and exhibition activities. However, the program goes beyond administrative structures and procedures to comply with federal regulations. The humane care and useofanimalsisasharedmissionandeverypersonat FAU(staff,faculty,or student) is expected to understand, implement, and maintain best practices for promoting and protecting animal welfare.

  3. Policy

    The FAU Animal Care and Use Program (ACUP) is a University-wide program with multiple components, which is depicted in detail in Appendix 1, Organizational Chart.

    Required components of the Animal Care and Use Program:
    1. The Institutional Official. Federal regulations require that institutions appoint an Institutional Official (IO) who has ultimate authority and accountability for the animal care and use program, including allocating fiscal and non-fiscal resources to the program and ensuring compliance with the regulations. At FAU, the President has delegated that role to the Vice- President for Research. Although the overall direction of the ACUP is a shared responsibility among the IO, Attending Veterinarian (AV), and Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC), the IO is the person who is ultimately held responsible by federal authorities. For t h a t reason, the IO has the authority to withhold approval for any animal activity, even if it has been approved by the IACUC; however, the IO may not approve any activity where the IACUC has withheld approval.
    2. The Attending Veterinarian (AV). The AV is responsible for the health and well-being of all animals used in research, training, and exhibition at FAU. This includes oversight of the following: a) animal procurement and transportation; b) preventative medicine (including quarantine, biosecurity, and surveillance); c) treatment of clinical disease, disability, or related health issues; d) assessment and care of protocol-associated disease, disability, and related outcomes; e) surgery and perioperative care; f) assessment of pain and distress; g) anesthesia and analgesia; and h) euthanasia. The AV is an ex officio voting member of the IACUC and is involved in the design, planning and review of the animal care and use protocol. The AV also manages other aspects of animal care and use such as husbandry, housing of animals, and specialized training. In this capacity, she also serves as Director of Comparative Medicine Services at FAU.
    3. The Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC). The IACUC is a faculty- led committee composed of scientists, non-scientists, veterinary professionals, and members of the community [community members have no affiliation with FAU]. The IACUC oversees and evaluates the animal care and use program by: a) reviewing all animal research, training, and exhibition projects, and making decisions to approve, withhold approval, require modifications, or suspend projects as appropriate; b) developing, reviewing and assessing all components of the animal program as well as inspecting and evaluating all animal facilities (including satellite facilities), laboratory space and study areas semi-annually and reporting these findings to the IO; c) reviewing and investigating legitimate concerns involving the care and use of animals at the institution and d) making recommendations to the IO regarding any aspect of the animal program, facilities or personnel training.

    Additional Administrative Components of the Animal Care and Use Program:

    The FAU ACUP (or “Program”) includes all “activities conducted by and at an institution that have a direct impact on the well-being of animals, including animal and veterinary care, policies and procedures, personnel and program management and oversight, occupational health and safety, IACUC functions, and animal facility design and management.” The extensive volume of paperwork and processes required by Federal regulations cannot be handled solely by the IO, AV, and IACUC. To administer a diverse Animal Care and Use Program that spans four campuses, FAU relies on the support and collaboration of the following administrative units:

    1. Research Integrity (RI). RI, located in the Division of Research, is the primary administrative support office for all research compliance programs at FAU, including the animal care and use program. RI supports the IO, IACUC, faculty and students through a variety of functions including: a) advising faculty and students on the protocol submission process; b) processing all animal protocols and related actions; c) coordinating and scheduling all meetings and facility inspections; d) issuing all correspondence from the IACUC and IO to faculty and students; e) interacting with federal agencies (e.g., OLAW and UDSA) to discuss and report any compliance issues; f) investigatinganimalwelfareandnon-compliancecaseson behalfofthe IACUC; g) performing post-approval monitoring; and h) performing other related actions. RI also coordinates all general training programs related to animal care and use, and works closely with the AV and CM staff who provide specialized training directed by the IACUC. RI facilitates communications between all components of the animal care and use program including the IO, AV, IACUC, and the FAU community.

    2. Comparative Medicine (CM). According to the Guide for the Care and Use of Animals, an institution must provide its AV with sufficient authority, including access to all animals and resources to manage the program of veterinary care. FAU’s Office of Comparative Medicine, also located in the Division of Research, provides these core resources. Comparative Medicine staff support the Director of Comparative Medicine by routinely observing animals for signs of pain or distress, assisting in veterinary medical care, providing husbandry to animals (feeding, breeding, enrichment programs, etc.), and maintaining housing and facilities for all vertebrate animals on FAU campuses. At the direction of the IACUC, CM also provides “protocol-specific” training to personnel working with animals (see IACUC policy “10.4.22, Researcher Training”).

    3. Environmental Health and Safety (EH&S) & Facilities Management. EH&S is the primary contact at FAU for matters of health, safety and the environment. EH&S supports the FAU ACUP by managing the occupational health and safety program known as the Medical Monitoring Program for Animals Users (MMPAU). Through the MMPAU, faculty and students working with animals receive initial assessment and risk evaluation, referral to appropriate vaccinations and medical care, and ongoing monitoring of their health status. EH&S also manages efforts to minimize biological, chemical and radioactive hazards associated with animal activities.

      Facilities Management oversees services such as construction, building maintenance, and physical plant operations. Facilities Management coordinates with the IO, AV, and IACUC to ensure any facility and physical plant issues (utilities, construction, noise control, etc.) do not adversely affect the well-being of animals at FAU. Both EH&S and Facilities Management report to FAU’s Vice-President for Administrative Affairs.

      Collectively, FAU’s IO, AV, IACUC in conjunction with the administrative offices noted above, work together to promote animal welfare at FAU.

The University...at a Glance

Some universities measure greatness in decades and centuries. Florida Atlantic University measures it with every student who earns a degree, every researcher who makes a discovery and every community that is transformed.

At FAU's dedication ceremony in 1964, President Lyndon B. Johnson challenged the University's pioneers: "It is time now...for a new, adventurous, imaginative, courageous breakthrough for a new revolution in education in America."

With those words, FAU opened its doors as the first public university in southeast Florida and the first in America designed for upper division students only. Since day one, FAU has pushed the bounds of higher education. Now, 50 years later, the University serves more than 30,000 freshmen, transfers and graduate students at sites throughout its six-county service region in southeast Florida.

People from every walk of life find a place at FAU. Students choose from more than 170 degree programs, faculty researchers utilize more than 40 research centers and the community engages hundreds of cultural and educational events every year.
