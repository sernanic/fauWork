Subject:  Photography and Videography in Laboratory Animals
Effective Date:  1/27/2017
Policy Number:  10.4.27
Supersedes:  N/A
Responsible Authorities:  Vice President, Research Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee Director, Research Integrity Director, Comparative Medicine

  1. Background

    There are scientific advantages for the use of photographs or videos of animals for research, teaching or testing purposes. However, with the prevalent use of social media and access to mobile devices with cameras and video recorders photographs or videos may be used out of context by groups opposed to research involving animals in an effort to misrepresent humane and ethical animal use as inappropriate.

  2. Purpose

    The purpose of this policy is to establish guidance to Research personnel and Comparative Medicine personnel regarding photography and videotaping of animals within animal facilities and Investigator laboratories as described in approved IACUC protocols and to guard against the misinterpretation of appropriate and humane policies and procedures.

  3. General Statement

    FAU and its Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee are committed to protecting the confidentiality and integrity of animal research, to protect the privacy and safety interests of research personnel and staff and to protect the health and welfare of research animals.

  4. Policy

    1. Any photographs or videos taken of animals within FAU animal facilities are prohibited unless documented on an approved IACUC protocol or amendment.

    2. Distribution of photographs or videos of animals are not permitted to be posted to any type of social media unless approved by the IACUC.

    3. Only research personnel listed on the approved IACUC protocol that describe the use of photography and videography are permitted to take photographs or videos.

    4. The Principal Investigator must approve all photographs or videos taken of animals on the approved IACUC protocol.

    5. Comparative medicine staff are permitted to use photographs or videos for veterinary medical purposes including wildlife and biomedical clinical documentation and training. An approved IACUC protocol is not required for veterinary clinical care.

    6. Photographs or videos must be stored on a secure password protected computer or server and the original image must be deleted immediately from the image device.

  5. Definitions

    Images are any photograph or video recording (digital or analog). Devices include cameras, video recorders, audio recorders, computer-mounted video capture units, and mobile cellphone or tablet cameras.

    Animal Facility is an IACUC designated place where animals are housed under an approved IACUC protocol, either a CM managed centralized vivarium (including procedure space) or a PI managed satellite facility or approved study area.

    Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) refers to protective clothing including hair covers, eye/face protection and gloves worn by faculty, staff and students for protection against health and safety hazards.

  6. Accountability

      The Principal Investigator (PI) will be responsible for:

    • Obtaining IACUC approval for the use of photographs or videos through an IACUC protocol.

    • Assuring that all Research personnel understand and follow this policy.

    • Approving all photographs or videos taken of animals.

    • Assuring high standards for the health and well-being of animals used in photographs or videos.

      • The Office of Comparative Medicine (CM) will be responsible for:

    • Assuring that all Comparative Medicine personnel understand and follow this policy.

    • Approving all photographs or videos taken of animals.

    • Assuring high standards for the health and well-being of animals used in photographs or videos.

      • The IACUC will be responsible for:

    • Reviewing and approving, requiring modifications in (to secure approval) or withholding approval of IACUC protocols/amendments of the procedures described for photography and videography of animals.

    • Providing oversight and guidance for all animal procedures conducted including the use of photography and videography of animals.

      • The Research Integrity office will be responsible for:

    • Administratively support IACUC members to facilitate their regulatory function.

    • Maintaining policy and assure regular review and update as necessary by the IACUC.

  7. Procedures

    1. Procedures shown in the photograph or video must be an approved method described in the protocol.

    2. Appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) must be worn by all personnel.

    3. Appropriate methods of handling and restraint must be used while photographing or videotaping animals.

    4. Animals must have clean surroundings or cages with clean accessories. Water bottles and feeders must be filled if shown in the photograph or video.

    5. Photographs or videos that include animals with visible lesions or research alterations (i.e. implants, tumors) should be cropped to center on the lesion or research alteration instead of the animal.

    6. No references to personal information may be visible in the photograph or video. This includes displaying any locations, names of personnel, institutions, buildings, room numbers or cage cards.

    7. All personnel must be unidentified when visible in the photograph or video.

Some universities measure greatness in decades and centuries. Florida Atlantic University measures it with every student who earns a degree, every researcher who makes a discovery and every community that is transformed.

With those words, FAU opened its doors as the first public university in southeast Florida and the first in America designed for upper division students only. Since day one, FAU has pushed the bounds of higher education. Now, 50 years later, the University serves more than 30,000 freshmen, transfers and graduate students at sites throughout its six-county service region in southeast Florida.

People from every walk of life find a place at FAU. Students choose from more than 170 degree programs, faculty researchers utilize more than 40 research centers and the community engages hundreds of cultural and educational events every year.
