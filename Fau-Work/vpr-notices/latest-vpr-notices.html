Re:        Foreign Threats

Date:     July 27, 2019

Florida Atlantic University (FAU) has a long history of successful collaboration with foreign research organizations and supports and encourages international research, collaboration, and scholarship, which contribute to the mission of the University.

In recent months, the National Institutes of Health (NIH) and other U.S. federal agencies, (NSF, DOD, and DOE) that support university research have demonstrated concern over foreign influence and have issued guidance to universities to ensure transparency on financial support received from all foreign entities.

The first line of defense is full and accurate reporting of all foreign relationships by U.S. university researchers to their institution. This involves mandatory full disclosure of other financial support, when requested in proposal submission must include any financial support received by the investigator from a foreign entity outside the US. This disclosure includes support/funding received through contracts and grants through FAU or consultant, visiting scholar, lecture or similar non-FAU appointment. For faculty on nine-month appointments, this requirement includes applicable funds received from a non-FAU foreign entity for activities over the summer months.

An issue of special concern is participation by U.S. universities in foreign talent programs, such as China’s Thousand Talents Program, which the Pentagon has identified as an effort “to facilitate the legal and illicit transfer of US technology, intellectual property and know-how” to China.

The Division of Research is establishing a Task Force (or committee) to focus on foreign influence/threat, review current policies and procedures to ensure compliance and develop an implementation plan for FAU. Members of this committee will represent different interest groups across the university such as Provost’s office, OIT, HR and including faculty researchers. The DOR is in the process of creating a webpage that will provide pertinent information and the federal agencies notices. DOR will also be scheduling Town Hall meetings to further distribute information and guidance related to this essential topic.

All federal agencies are taking this issue very seriously. The best advice at this point is to be sure to disclose any and all activities that involve a foreign entity. Thank you for giving your full attention to the critically important issue of preventing foreign exploitation of U.S. research discoveries. Stay tuned for additional resources and information.

Re:        Division of Research, Human Research Protections Program – AAHRPP Accreditation

Date:     June 5, 2019

Florida Atlantic University promotes the highest code of conduct for research involving human subjects to adequately protect the rights and welfare of human subjects participating in that research. The Vice President for Research is Florida Atlantic University’s Presidential designee and the registered Institutional Official with the respective federal oversight regulatory agencies.

As you know, the FAU Division of Research (DoR) facilitates research involving human subjects through its Institutional Review Board (IRB), a federally mandated committee which has oversight of all policies, procedures and practices relating to institutional review and oversight of research protocols involving human subjects. http://www.fau.edu/research/research-integrity/irb-policies-and-procedures.php

FAU’s Division of Research is consistently working on enhancing the human research protection program (HRPP) through quality improvement initiatives designed to facilitate research, ensure regulatory compliance while minimizing administrative burdens, and strengthen protections for research participants. This memorandum is to inform you that FAU is seeking accreditation from the Association for the Accreditation of Human Research Protections Programs (AAHRPP), a highly respected, independent, non-profit accrediting body. AAHRPP accreditation will assure research participants, researchers, sponsors, government regulators and the general public of the quality of FAU’s research involving human subjects. This will not only increase the esteem for FAU’s research among its peer institutions but will also increase FAU’s ability to obtain research funding. Accreditation is a voluntary, peer driven, educationally based model that recognizes high quality HRPPs of research institutions in the areas of organization, IRB, researcher compliance and research personnel administrative oversight. This effort will enable FAU to achieve the highest standard of accreditation with a comprehensive set of professional and ethical standards and practices that signify the commitment and quality of FAU’s human research protections program and the commitment of FAU’s researchers and administration to that program.

The DoR will be working with researchers and the Colleges as it endeavors to obtain AAHRPP accreditation. As a reminder, before researchers submit research protocols to the DoR, it is required that the researcher’s college or unit review and approve the following elements of each research protocol: scientific merit, methodology, project personnel, feasibility and resources (equipment and infrastructure), recognizing that other matters may arise requiring college or unit approval depending on the research proposal. I look forward to your joining the DoR in this effort to obtain AAHRPP accreditation.

Re:        Division of Research, Human Research Protections Program – AAHRPP Accreditation

Date:     May 21, 2019

This memorandum serves as a reminder to the FAU research community that the Division of Research (DoR) reviews all matters related to the conduct of University research. Allegations of research misconduct (fabrication, falsification and plagiarism) are managed by a prescriptive regulatory process that involves ad-hoc faculty committee peer review of the allegation(s). It is the DoR’s charge to manage all such assessments, inquiries and investigations in accordance with state and federal laws, regulations, and DoR Policy 10.1.1

The Vice President for Research is Florida Atlantic University’s Presidential designee and the registered Institutional Official and Research Integrity Officer with the respective federal regulatory agencies. Should faculty, staff or students have concerns related to the conduct of research, such concerns should be brought to the attention of the DoR.

The DoR has primary responsibility for managing the review of potential research misconduct, but it discharges its responsibilities in coordination with other units of the University. For example, the Provost is FAU’s designated Deciding Official who decides, in consultation with the Vice President for Research, whether allegations of misconduct warrant an investigation and what actions are appropriate upon a finding of misconduct. Additionally, before researchers may submit research proposals to the DoR, it is required that the researcher’s college or unit review and approve the following elements of any proposal: scientific merit, methodology, project personnel, feasibility and resources (equipment and infrastructure) and other required necessities to successfully and safely execute proposed research studies. Such review and approval prior to submission of proposals should help minimize the potential for research misconduct.

This coordination of its activities also places limits on the DoR’s institutional roles. For example, the DoR does not have purview of matters related to academic impropriety or student discipline, or to supervisory and personnel matters (except those relating to employees of the Division of Research). The DoR is available to assist other units of the University in addressing such matters if warranted, but it does not have primary responsibility in such instances except to the extent that potential research misconduct is identified.
